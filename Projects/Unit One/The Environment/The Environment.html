Home

The many advances in the technology industry are the cause of both many problems and betterments for the environment. When considering digital technology, such as computers and phones, their popularity has increased the demand for energy, changed how we communicate and interact with each other, and created an entirely new layer to everyday life. These changes have many sub-problems that require further exploration in order to develop a full understanding of the implications of technology. Although technology has negative impacts on the environment, it also allows for more education and research which can help to minimize these impacts [1]. By providing information on the problems associated with technology, as well as the solutions, this website is an example of how technology can be used to protect our environment.

In the Problem tab, a few of the many impacts that technology has on the environment and human health are discussed: the consumption of energy, waste management, and computer-related physical health issues.

The Statistics tab presents data relating to the issues discussed in the problems tab. These help to visualize the severity of what we are dealing with.

The Initiatives tab explains what is being done by the government and other organizations to prevent and solve the issues that technology has caused.

The Act Now tab suggests different ways that humans can help prevent the issues associated with technology.

The References tab provides a list of the works referenced throughout the site in IEEE format.

The Problem

Energy Consumption
The acceleration of digitalization has led to higher demands for energy [1]. Accordingly, this has increased carbon emissions and the depletion of natural resources [1].
Waste Management
The materials needed for digital devices are mined by using harmful chemicals such as mercury [2]. These chemicals are then dumped into the ground, polluting the environment and affecting the health of the people living nearby. Additionally, many people incorrectly dispose of their digital devices and their components. This further harms the environment and poses a health risk [2].
Physical Health
Many jobs require people to use computers for extended periods of time, especially given the COVID-19 pandemic. Poor working environments and unergonomic conditions can lead to bad posture, muscle strain, and tendon injuries [3].

Statistics

Despite common belief, the internet has a very large carbon footprint [4]. In 2019, the world’s collective digital carbon footprint accounted for nearly 3.7% of all greenhouse emissions [5].
Given the size of this website, it is estimated that it releases 0.3 grams of carbon on every load.
Manufacturing a regular computer takes around 3 times its weight in fossil fuels [1].
630 grams of fossil fuels are used to manufacture 1 gram of a silicon wafer [1].
1,5 kg of fuels and chemicals are required for 2-gram microchips [1].
It takes approximately 1,5 tons of water to manufacture a personal computer [1].
Only about 20–30% of all toner waste is recycled [7].

Initiatives

There are various electrical and electronic equipment waste-management regulations that have been adopted to minimise negative environmental impact.

Ontario’s Resource Recovery and Circular Economy Act, for example, demands that battery producers are responsible for the collection and end-of-life management of their products [8].

In 2017, The Centre for Green Chemistry & Green Engineering at Yale University developed a sustainable method to extract and reuse rare earth metals in electronic devices such as smartphones and computers [9].

There are many organisations, such as Insertech [10], that refurbish digital technology. This helps to reduce overconsumption, waste, and pollution.

Through the internet and social media, many people communicate and strategize on how to solve technology-related issues [11].

Act Now

  • Turn off technology such as computers, printers, and fax machines when they are not in use [12].
  • Buy devices that are refurbished or those made from recycled materials [12].
  • Locate a technology and battery waste management facility to dispose of your devices when they have reached the end of their life [12].
  • Limit the number of unnecessary emails you send. This can be made easier by downloading chrome extensions such as OVO’s Carbon Capper Chrome which notifies users when their email is under four words [6].
  • To minimise the carbon footprint of a website, ensure that the code is written efficiently, the fonts are web-safe, the host of the site uses renewable energy, and minimise the number of page-loads required [4].
  • Advocate for cleaner methods of energy, and influence governments. This can be done by protesting, signing petitions, contacting government representatives, and educating others [13].
